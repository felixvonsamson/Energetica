# Wiki - Power Management

Power management lets you control which facilities generate or consume electricity, and in what order. The rules that determine how much power each facility produces are covered in [the Power Facilities wiki](./power-facilities.mdx#power-generation-of-renewable-facilities).

Power management becomes available once you unlock the **network achievement** (generating more than 3 MW). Before that, the game uses its own default priorities.

## The Power Priorities List

Once unlocked, you manage priorities through an interactive list on the <LockedLink to="/app/facilities/power-priorities" capability="has_network">Power Priorities</LockedLink> page. This list includes every facility and process that produces or consumes electricity. Storage facilities appear twice — once as a generator (discharge) and once as a consumer (charge).

The list follows two simple rules:

- **Generators:** Higher priority = lower in the list. A generator only supplies power to consumers placed above it.
- **Consumers:** Higher priority = higher in the list. A consumer only accepts power from generators placed below it.

Renewable facilities (wind, solar, hydro) are always fixed at the bottom of the list. Their output is non-controllable and always consumed first.

A generator placed at the very **top** of the list will never be used, since no consumers are above it.

## Prices & the Electricity Market

Each facility in the list also has a price attached to it. The list is always ordered from highest to lowest price.

If you are not part of an electricity market, this price is just a priority number, it defines the order of the elements in the list.

If you are part of an electricity market however, prices become meaningful:

- For a **generator**: the minimum price at which you're willing to sell its power.
- For a **consumer**: the maximum price you're willing to pay for the power it uses.

The prices and available capacities of all players together determine the supply and demand curves shown on the merit order chart on the <LockedLink to="/app/overviews/electricity-markets" capability="has_network">Electricity Market</LockedLink> page.

## Editing the List

You have two ways to reorder elements:

- Click the arrows next to an element to move it one position up or down. Note that this also swaps the prices of the two elements.
- Change a price directly. The element automatically moves to the correct position in the list.

Changes take effect after the game processes the next tick.
